Every year, thousands of computers reach end-of-lease and are wiped, warehoused, or recycled — while K-12 students in under-resourced schools have never touched a machine they're actually allowed to use.

OpenKeys closes that gap. We collect donated hardware and grant it — permanently, without restrictions — to schools building real computer science programs. A gift-in-kind receipt for your hardware. A tax deduction for your cash. And a direct line from your generosity to a student learning to code for the first time.

"To bridge the digital divide in K-12 education by providing unrestricted computers to schools — empowering students with real-world computing access."

— OpenKeys Mission Statement

How your contribution is used

🔧

Refurb & prep

Wiped, tested, and configured with Linux, Python, and VS Code.

🏫

Matched to schools

Granted to Title I and under-resourced schools with active CS programs.

📊

Impact reported

Schools submit annual updates — you see exactly where your gift landed.

Ways to give

💻

Donate hardware

Laptops or desktops 3–7 years old, capable of running Linux. End-of-lease fleets welcome. We issue gift-in-kind receipts for fair market value — fully tax-deductible.
💵

Make a monetary gift

Fund device sourcing, refurbishment, and logistics directly. Processed through our fiscal sponsor with max 5% platform overhead. 100% tax-deductible.
🤝

Corporate or grant partnership

Recurring device pipelines, CSR matching programs, and foundation grants (Google.org, Microsoft Philanthropies, etc.) are all welcome. Reply to discuss tailored arrangements.
